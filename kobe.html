Kobe Bryant (August 23, 1978, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania â€“ January 26, 2020, Calabasas, California) was an American basketball player. He played in the Los Angeles Lakers NBA franchise for twenty seasons, between 1996 and 2016. Five-time NBA champion, he is one of seven players to have scored more than 30,000 career points. He is also a double Olympic champion with the United States team, in 2008 and 2012. He is considered one of the best basketball players of all time. Selected in 13th position in the 1996 NBA Draft by the Charlotte Hornets, when he was a high school student, he was immediately transferred to the Los Angeles Lakers. After several apprenticeship seasons in the NBA, he formed with Shaquille O'Neal one of the most successful duos in the history of the American League, achieving a historic hat-trick, with three consecutive NBA titles in 2000, 2001 and 2002 . In 2003, he was accused of sexual assault and rape of a nineteen-year-old young woman. Bryant publicly apologized in 2004 After a failure in the 2004 finals, he became the leader of his team and achieved quality individual performances, although he was then criticized for his lack of altruism. On January 22, 2006, he scored 81 points against the Toronto Raptors, the second-highest single-game point total in NBA history.
